# AML Domain Rules

- AML is risk-based detection, not deterministic classification.
- Typologies of interest include layering, smurfing/structuring, fan-in, fan-out, chain, and cycle-like flows.
- A community is only a candidate suspicious subgraph, not final truth.
- A motif is only suspicious when topology, timing, amount, repetition, and context align.
- Do not assume fixed global thresholds; thresholds must be configurable per use case.
- Do not equate one structural pattern with laundering without temporal and monetary context.
- Analyst feedback and confirmed cases are evaluation signals, not perfect ground truth for all settings.
